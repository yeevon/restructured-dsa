## What and why

The TASK-005 12-Chapter screenshot review surfaced ~600–800 visible LaTeX-bleed defects across 5 categories. TASK-007 cleared ~250+ defects in the two highest-impact bounded categories (tabular column-spec residue + `\texttt{}` math-trap). This task is the next bounded follow-up: it clears the largest remaining bounded category — unhandled `\begin{...}` environments and bleed-through text-formatting macros — totaling **~127 visible instances** per the TASK-005 catalog (Gap A: ~28 instances, ch-09 dominant with 22; Gap B: ~99 instances, ch-10 dominant with 60).

The two sub-gaps are filed under one project_issue because both surface from the same parser-walker fall-through path:

1. **Gap A — unhandled `\begin{<env>}` / `\end{<env>}` tokens leak through as literal text.** The parser's structural walker (`_nodes_to_html`) routes known environments (callouts, `tabular`, `lstlisting`, `itemize`, `enumerate`, `equation`, etc.) to their handlers. The `else` branch at `app/parser.py:874–883` logs a warning and recursively renders `inner_nodelist` — but for environments like `algorithm`, `algorithmic`, `proof`, `theorem`, `figure`, the literal `\begin{<env>}` and `\end{<env>}` tokens reach the rendered HTML body. Most concentrated in ch-09 (balanced trees → algorithm/proof environments).

2. **Gap B — text-formatting macros `\textbf` / `\textit` / `\emph` / `\textsc` (and possibly bare `\texttt` without an argument) leak through with their wrapper tokens visible.** Inline handlers exist (`app/parser.py:143–150`); the bleed therefore happens at call sites that bypass `_convert_inline_latex` — most likely inside the unknown-environment fallback (Gap A's recursion path emits the inner content via `_nodes_to_html`, which routes inline content through `flush_para → _convert_inline_latex`, but the unhandled `\begin{algorithm}` token itself sits in the body as a `LatexCharsNode` that escapes to literal HTML), or inside other handlers that escape raw verbatim. The `/design` cycle's first task is to confirm the actual leak path before picking the fix shape.

Together these clear the largest remaining bounded category in one session, following the TASK-004 / TASK-007 precedent (parser-handler change + ADR + cross-corpus re-verification against the TASK-005 12-chapter Playwright harness).

This advances the primary objective (manifest §3) directly: every visible LaTeX-bleed in the rendered Lecture surface degrades the consumption signal. With Lecture rendering now substantially polished (TASK-001 → TASK-007), the remaining bounded parser-fidelity categories deliver real-but-diminishing consumption uplift — see "Architectural concerns I want to raise" for the strategic-balance question this task does not resolve.

## Architectural decisions expected

- **ADR-NNN: Unhandled-environment rendering strategy.** The project_issue enumerates three options: (1) per-environment explicit handlers, (2) generic unknown-env fallback that emits `<div class="unrecognized-env" data-env="X">…</div>` + warn-per-node, (3) hybrid (explicit handlers for high-frequency cases, generic fallback for the rest). The architect picks at `/design`. **Architect's forecast: Option 3 hybrid** — explicit handlers for the top-frequency unhandled envs identified in the `/design`-time corpus walk (likely `algorithm`, `algorithmic`, `proof`, `theorem`, `figure`), generic fallback for the long tail. Per-environment editorial intent (e.g., `algorithm` → `<div class="algorithm">` vs. `<pre>` vs. `<figure>`) needs decision. Aligned with ADR-012's precedent for emitting semantic structural HTML rather than passthrough.
- **ADR-NNN (possibly the same ADR, possibly a sibling): Text-formatting-macro bleed root-cause fix.** The architect's `/design` cycle should first **confirm the leak path** — Gap B's actual mechanism is not certain pre-`/design`. The fix shape depends on whether the leak is from (i) inside the unknown-env recursion path (fix follows from Gap A's resolution), (ii) a bypass of `_convert_inline_latex` somewhere else in the walker, or (iii) macros appearing in a context that has its own rendering path (e.g., section titles, captions). The architect commits to: (a) reproduce the leak in a test, (b) trace the call site, (c) decide whether one ADR covers both gaps or two ADRs separate them.

These become Proposed ADRs during `/design TASK-008`. Nothing substantive lands in `architecture.md` until the human gates them.
